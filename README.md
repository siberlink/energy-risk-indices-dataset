# Global Energy Risk Indices Dataset (GERI, EERI, EGSI)

An open, structured dataset of **global energy risk indicators** designed to quantify **geopolitical risk, market stress, and supply-side disruptions** across global and European energy systems.

This repository provides **daily time-series data** for three proprietary indices:

- Global Energy Risk Index (GERI)  
- European Energy Risk Index (EERI)  
- Europe Gas Stress Index (EGSI)  

---

## What This Dataset Covers

Energy markets are no longer driven by supply-demand fundamentals alone.

They are increasingly shaped by:

- Geopolitical escalation  
- War and conflict risk  
- Energy infrastructure disruptions  
- LNG and gas flow constraints  
- Market volatility and sentiment  

This dataset captures these dynamics through **quantified risk indices** built to track and interpret systemic energy risk.

---

## Indices Overview

### GERI — Global Energy Risk Index
Measures **global geopolitical and systemic energy risk** on a normalized 0–100 scale.

Captures:
- Global conflict intensity
- Supply disruption risk
- Cross-market contagion
- System-wide escalation signals

---

### EERI — European Energy Risk Index
Tracks **escalation risk specific to European energy markets**.

Captures:
- Regional geopolitical developments
- EU energy system exposure
- Transmission of global risk into Europe
- Market-sensitive escalation signals

---

### EGSI — Europe Gas Stress Index
Measures **stress within the European gas system**, including physical and market factors.

Captures:
- Storage levels and refill risk
- LNG flows and supply dependency
- Pipeline disruptions and chokepoints
- Market volatility (TTF and related signals)
